Electric Stove On/Off Detector

Introduction: This project is an app where you can check if your stove is on or off. It was first thought of as a solution to when my my mother'solution issue with when she leaves home she always worry her stove is on.

Specifics and Steps

I began researching possible solutions in the spring of 2025. In the begginin, I considered installing a camera to monitor the stove, similar to home security systems. However, this approach had several problems: It was difficult and expensive to install above the stove Oil and smoke residue from cooking could cover the camera requiring regular cleaning Large data transmission required So I rejected this idea. Next, I thought about using a temperature sensor, but it was also difficult to install in a kitchen and there wasn't really a good place to put it. Other than that I thought of directly installing a device into the stove to check if it is getting electricity but I scrapped the idea as it was impratical on a lot of levels namely the safety warnings on modification safety, little space to fit something in the stove, and having to take the stove partially apart to modify the device.. Finally, I came up with a better idea: Measure the electrical current of the stove using magnetissm. When the stove is ON → current is high When the stove is OFF → current is very low I needed a solution that could: Measure current Without changing the original circuit Safe and non-invasive From my self studying of physics, I remembered that AC current can be detected using electromagnetic induction. This led me to explore non-contact current sensors. After further research, I selected sensors based on my stove’s specifications: Brand: Whirlpool Power: 9.6 kW Voltage: 240V Using the formula: P=V⋅I I calculated the current: I=9600W/240V=40A So I focused on sensors that could measure with the maximum 40A current. 3. Sensor Testing and Selection I tested three different sensors over two months. (1) First Sensor: SCT-013-050 Non-contact current transformer Output: 0–1V Problems: Output voltage difference was too small Difficult to distinguish stove ON/OFF Not truly easy to install (needed to pass wire through sensor) Rejected due to low sensitivity and installation difficulty. (2) Second Sensor: SZT20-420E Output: AC signal (changing continuously) Problems: Output signal unstable Weak signal with high error Hard to process data Rejected due to unstable output. (3) Final Sensor: QNCTK3-16 Output: stable DC voltage (0–5V) Non-contact clamp design Advantages: Stable output Easy installation Clear voltage change based on stove usage
